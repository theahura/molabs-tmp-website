Diabetic Retinopathy

More then 30.3 million people suffer from diabetes here in the United States. A quarter of them suffer from diabetic retinopathy, and this number is only growing.

Diabetic retinopathy(DR) is a retinal disease that affects patients with diabetes. It is currently diagnosed entirely by fundoscopic analysis but manual analysis of DR is time-consuming and not always accessible. Furthermore, early diagnosis of DR in its non-proliferative stage is difficult due to a lack of outward visible symptoms and patients failing to attend regular ophthalmology appointments, resulting in under diagnosis and the onset of advanced complications.

Diabetic retinopathy can lead to a number of complications in eyesight; patients may suffer from a vitreous hemorrhage leading into a macular edema. This may result in a partial loss of eyesight or blindness.

Between 40 to 45% of Americans diagnosed with diabetes have some stage of diabetic retinopathy and this disease accounts for at least 12% of all new annual cases of blindness in the United States alone

So what are we proposing?

Our Solution

By combining a hardware attachment to your average smartphone with a machine learning model trained to detect retinopathy, we hope to develop a device patients and optometrists can use to identify and monitor the onsset of retinopathy

Our camera module will consist of a lens system combined with an infrared focusing module that should be able to fit onto a smartphone. This will allow patients to use their phones as a basic fundoscope and then upload the image to our analysis platform as necessary.

For analysis of the captured images, a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) will be used. A CNN operates by training on a large number of labeled images in order to extract important features that define a classification. After training the model, the CNN can then make predictions about the category of new input images.

Our team hopes to use this novel solution to make sure screening reaches those who need it most.
